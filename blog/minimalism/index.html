What is Minimalism?

In a simple term, Minimalism is a lifestyle or design philosophy that focuses on keeping things simple and clutter-free. It means owning and using only what you truly need and love, while removing unnecessary distractions or excess. Minimalism promotes a sense of clarity, intentionality, and peace by emphasizing quality over quantity and simplifying various aspects of life, such as possessions, space, and even daily routines. It’s about living with less, but finding more meaning and happiness in what you have.

Minimalism is a life concept I embrace. Even though the term contains “minimal” it doesn’t imply that we should have few possessions or adhere to a “less is better” philosophy.

Embracing minimalism means only having things that hold value for us. For instance, if we want to own ten pieces of clothing, and these clothes add value to our lives, that’s perfectly fine; it aligns with the essence of this concept.

Instead of merely possessing things (like cars or gadgets) to seek attention and approval from others, we should ensure that our belongings are genuinely valuable to us and fulfill our needs; this lies at the core of minimalism.

This approach relates to finding serenity in life. The more I accumulate things solely to impress others, the more I inadvertently hinder my own happiness. I cannot make everyone accept me, as my happiness should not depend on the validation of others, this is my fundamental belief.

By practicing minimalism, I find contentment within myself, and the quantity of possessions becomes less important. This way of life influences everything, from my thoughts and behavior to my interactions in society. The key is to attribute meaning to the things we own. Moreover, this concept is not limited to material possessions; it can apply to friendships too. Ensure that we value and share a sense of connection with our friends.

The goal of this lifestyle is to minimize disruptions in our life, whether they are negative thoughts we don’t need, dependence on others’ opinions for happiness, or overwhelming choices. It also involves distancing oneself from hurtful comments when we know the truth.

Minimalism allows me to act without unnecessary burdens, as long as the actions are positive. It enables me to accept things I cannot change, simplifying the complexity of life. It is a wisdom I continue to cultivate.
